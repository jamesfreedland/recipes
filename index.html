  • 1 1/2 cups honey (one honeybear)
  • Small pinch kosher salt
  • Optional: 1/2 cup citrus juice, grated citrus zest or fresh ginger

Method

  1. Combine cranberries, honey and salt in a medium pot (frozen berries can go straight in). Bring to a boil, drop to medium, add 1/2 cup water.

Pickles & Marinated

Ingredients

  • 3 1/2 oz sea salt (about 1/3 cup + 1 Tbsp)
  • 1/2 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 8 cups bottled water
  • 1 1/2 lb kirby cucumbers, washed and dried
  • Few sprigs fresh dill, washed and dried
  • Optional: 1 tsp sugar, 3–4 garlic cloves, 2 Tbsp peppercorns

Method

  1. Bring salt, vinegar and water to a boil; simmer 5 minutes. Cool slightly.
  2. Pack cucumbers upright and tight in a container, coming within 1/2 inch of the top; tuck in the dill (and optional garlic/peppercorns).
  3. Fill to the top with brine; tap on the counter to release air bubbles. Keeps up to a few weeks refrigerated.

Ingredients

  • 2 lb firm, unwaxed kirby pickling cucumbers
  • 2 cloves spring garlic, sliced thin (optional)
  • 1 dill flower, or 5 sprigs fresh dill or 1 tsp dill seed (optional)
  • 1/2 tsp coriander seed (optional)
  • 1/2 jalapeño, seeded and slivered (optional)
  • 2 Tbsp salt

Method

  1. Soak cucumbers 30 minutes in ice water; slice off the blossom end (opposite the stem — or a little off both ends). Cut into spears or chunks if desired.
  2. Pack into clean quart jars with garlic, dill, coriander and jalapeño.
  3. Dissolve salt in 2 cups boiling water; add 2 cups ice (filtered if your water is chlorinated) and stir until cool. Pour over to cover.
  4. Cap loosely, set jars in a bowl (they may leak) and ferment on the counter — the brine bubbles lazily and turns cloudy. Taste after 3 days; go another day or two for more sour, then refrigerate. Keeps a month.

Method

  1. Brown the turkey and onion in oil, breaking up, ~5 minutes; into the slow cooker.

  4. Stir in 1/2 cup cheese, top with the rest; cover 10–15 minutes on high to melt. Serve with toppings.

Boil

Method

  1. Whisk warm water and yeast in a stand-mixer bowl; cover 5 minutes. Add flour, sugar and salt; beat on low with the dough hook 2 minutes — stiff, slightly dry dough.

  3. Rise in a greased, covered bowl 60–90 minutes until doubled.
  4. Line two sheets. Punch down, divide into 8, ball up, poke a 1.5–2-inch hole through each. Rest under a towel while the bath heats.
  5. Oven 425°F. Boil the 2 quarts water with honey whisked in; reduce to medium-high. Boil bagels 2–4 at a time, 1 minute per side.
  6. Egg-wash tops and sides; 4 per sheet.
  7. Bake 20–25 minutes, rotating halfway, until dark golden. Cool 20 minutes on the pans, then rack.
  8. Keep airtight a few days, or refrigerate up to a week.

Ingredients

Hazelnut crust

  • 1 1/4 cups/180 g raw hazelnuts
  • 1 cup/125 g rice flour
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1/2 cup/112 g sugar
  • 6 Tbsp/100 g softened butter, more as needed

Cranberry curd

  • 12 oz/340 g cranberries
  • 1 cup/225 g sugar
  • Peel (orange part only) and juice of 1 orange (~1/2 cup)
  • 4 oz/113 g softened butter (1 stick)
  • 2 eggs + 2 egg yolks

Method

  1. Roast hazelnuts at 325°F 10–15 minutes until skins crack; rub skins off in a towel, cool.
  2. Grind nuts with half the rice flour to coarse cornmeal; pulse in the rest with the salt.
  3. Cream sugar and butter until pale; work in the nut mixture (1–2 Tbsp extra butter or cold water if crumbly). Press into a 10-inch tart pan — half for the sides, half for the bottom. Prick; freeze 30 minutes.
  4. Bake at 350°F until lightly brown, ~15 minutes. Cool.
  5. Curd: simmer cranberries, sugar, orange juice and peel until popped, ~10 minutes. Mill or sieve (or blend then sieve for the most vibrant color); whisk the butter into the warm purée.
  6. Beat eggs and yolks; temper with a cup of the warm liquid, combine, and cook over low until nearly bubbling and thickened, ~10 minutes. Cool (or refrigerate up to 1 day, wrap pressed to the surface).
  7. Fill the shell, smooth, bake 10 minutes at 350°F to set. Cool on a rack. Room temp up to 2 days.

  • 2 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 generous Tbsp sugar
  • 1 tsp kosher salt
  • 1 cup (2 sticks) butter, 1/2-inch cubes, frozen 15 minutes
  • 1/2 cup ice water + 1 tsp white vinegar

Filling

  • 2 1/2 lb firm baking apples (mix Fuji, Honeycrisp, Granny Smith), peeled, sliced
  • 1/2 cup + 1 Tbsp packed dark brown sugar
  • 3 Tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 tsp cinnamon; 1/4 tsp allspice
  • 1/2 tsp kosher salt
  • 1 Tbsp apple cider vinegar

Finish

  • 1 egg beaten with 1 Tbsp cream, for brushing
  • Granulated sugar, for sprinkling

Method

  1. Dough: whisk flour, sugar and salt. Work the frozen butter in until you have pea-size bits and inch-long shingles — the shingles are the flake.
  2. Stir the vinegar into the ice water; drizzle in gradually, stirring, stopping when a pinched handful holds together but still looks shaggy (you won't need it all). Divide, flatten into two discs, wrap, chill 2+ hours.
  3. Filling: toss the apple slices with brown sugar, flour, spices and salt; add the cider vinegar last.
  4. Heat oven to 425°F with a sheet pan on the bottom rack. Roll one disc to a 12-inch round, line a 9-inch plate, trim to a 1-inch overhang; freeze 10 minutes.
  5. Fill, top with the second round, tuck the top edge under the bottom, crimp, and cut 4–6 vents. Egg-wash and sugar the top.
  6. Bake 15 minutes at 425°F, then 45–60 at 375°F until the vents bubble thickly and the crust is deep gold (tent edges if needed).
  7. Cool at least 3 hours before slicing — that's what sets the filling.

Ingredients

  • 2 sticks (225 g) unsalted butter, room temp
  • 1 1/2 cups (300 g) granulated sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 1/3 cups (225 g) all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup (45 g) corn flour
  • 2/3 cup (65 g) freeze-dried corn powder (blitz freeze-dried corn)
  • 3/4 tsp baking powder; 1/4 tsp baking soda
  • 1 1/2 tsp kosher salt

Method

  1. Cream butter and sugar 2–3 minutes. Add the egg and beat a full 7–8 minutes — Tosi's signature step; it goes pale and fluffy.
  2. On low, add both flours, corn powder, leaveners and salt; mix under a minute, just to combine.
  3. Scoop big 2 3/4-oz domes (a 1/3-cup measure), flatten the tops, and chill at least 1 hour (up to a week). Never bake at room temp — they'll spread flat.
  4. Bake 4 inches apart at 350°F for ~18 minutes: cracked, spread, faintly brown at the rim, still bright yellow in the middle.
  5. Cool completely on the pans. Tastes like the platonic cornflake.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups (300 g) all-purpose flour
  • 1 Tbsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp kosher salt; 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 3/4 cup buttermilk or thin yogurt
  • 3/4 cup (180 g) sugar, plus more for the tops
  • 1/4 cup lightly packed light brown sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup (1 stick) melted butter or olive oil
  • Optional: 1 Tbsp lemon zest, 1/4 tsp cinnamon or 1/2 tsp vanilla
  • 2 1/2 cups blueberries, fresh or frozen (don't thaw)

Method

  1. Heat oven to 425°F; line or grease 12 muffin cups.
  2. Whisk the dry ingredients in one bowl. In the other, whisk both sugars with the eggs, then the buttermilk and butter.
  3. Fold dry into wet gently — a few floury streaks are fine. Fold in 2 cups of the berries.
  4. Fill the cups high, top with remaining berries and a generous crunch of sugar.
  5. Bake until domed, deeply golden and cooked through, 17–20 minutes.
  6. Sits opposite the Jordan Marsh muffins on the board: this one's craggy-topped and eggy; that one's the mashed-berry classic. Arguably better the next day.
